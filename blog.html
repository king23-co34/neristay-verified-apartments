How to Choose a Verified Short Let Apartment in Abuja

Abuja's short let market has grown fast, which means listings now range from genuinely secure, well-run apartments to units that look nothing like their photos once you arrive. Before you book a short let apartment in Abuja, run through a short checklist so you're not the one finding out the hard way.

Confirm the compound is gated and monitored. A short-term apartment is only as safe as the compound around it. Ask whether the property sits behind a secured gate, whether there's CCTV, and who has access after hours.

Ask about power supply before you ask about price. Steady electricity and backup power matter more in Abuja than almost anywhere else, especially if you're working remotely or staying for a medical visit. A verified listing should be upfront about this, not vague.

Check that photos match a real, recent inspection. Listings that are "verified" should mean someone physically inspected the apartment recently — not that photos were uploaded once, years ago. At Neristay, every apartment marked Verified by Neri has been inspected by our team for security, cleanliness and accuracy before it goes live.

Get a real phone number, not just a chat window. A short let agency that's reachable by phone or WhatsApp — and answers — is a strong signal of a business that will actually help you if something goes wrong during your stay.

If you'd rather skip the guesswork entirely, every apartment on Neristay is pre-verified, gated, and comes with steady power and 24/7 support. Check availability for your dates.

Best Areas for Short Let Apartments Near Abuja CBD

Where you should stay in Abuja depends heavily on why you're visiting. Here's a quick breakdown of the areas guests ask us about most.

Central Business District (CBD). If you're in Abuja for meetings, government business or conferences, staying inside or near the CBD — like our featured apartment on Presidential Boulevard — cuts commute time dramatically and puts you close to banks, offices and major hotels.

Maitama & Asokoro. Quieter, more residential, and popular with guests who want a calmer short-term stay while still being a short drive from the CBD — a good fit for longer relocation stays.

Wuse & Garki. Central and well-connected, with easy access to restaurants, hospitals and shopping — a practical middle ground for business or medical-visit guests who want convenience without CBD pricing.

Wherever you land, the same rule applies: prioritise a gated, verified compound over a slightly cheaper listing with no security information. Read our FAQ for more on how we vet locations before listing them.

Short Let vs Hotel in Abuja: What's Actually Cheaper?

On a single-night basis, a hotel room and a short let apartment in Abuja can look similarly priced. The difference shows up once you factor in space, length of stay and what's actually included.

Space and privacy. A short let apartment typically gives you a separate living area, a full kitchen and sometimes multiple bedrooms — useful if you're travelling with family or colleagues and want to split one booking instead of paying for multiple hotel rooms.

Cost per night on longer stays. Hotels rarely discount much beyond a few nights. Short let apartments, including ours, typically offer better weekly and monthly rates — which matters if you're in Abuja for relocation, a project, or an extended medical visit.

What's included. A verified short let should include steady power, WiFi and housekeeping options as standard — check this before comparing price against a hotel that bundles breakfast or a gym you won't use.

For most stays beyond two or three nights, a verified short let apartment in Abuja works out both cheaper and more comfortable than a comparable hotel room. Get a quote for your dates and compare for yourself.
